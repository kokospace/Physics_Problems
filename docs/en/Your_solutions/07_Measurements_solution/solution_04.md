# 🚗 Relative Uncertainty – Speedometer Example

## Problem
A car's speedometer has a **5% uncertainty**.  
If the speedometer reads **60 km/h**, what is the range of the car's actual speed?

---

## Step 1 — Calculate the Uncertainty

The uncertainty is 5% of 60 km/h.

Formula:

`Uncertainty = Percentage × Measured Value`

Calculation:

`0.05 × 60 = 3 km/h`

So the speed may vary by **±3 km/h**.

---

## Step 2 — Find the Speed Range

### Minimum Possible Speed

`60 - 3 = 57 km/h`

### Maximum Possible Speed

`60 + 3 = 63 km/h`

---

# ✅ Final Answer

`57 km/h ≤ Actual Speed ≤ 63 km/h`

The car's actual speed is between:

# **57 km/h and 63 km/h**
