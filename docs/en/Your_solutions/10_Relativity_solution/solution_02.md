# Time Dilation Problem

Two identical devices are built and synchronized on Earth.  
Each device plays 1-hour episodes of a TV show.

One device stays on Earth, while the other is placed on a spaceship moving at a relativistic speed.

We want the device on Earth to play **2 episodes**, while the device on the spaceship plays only **1 episode**.

## Given

Time measured on Earth:

$$
\Delta t = 2\ \text{hours}
$$

Time measured on the spaceship:

$$
\Delta \tau = 1\ \text{hour}
$$

The time dilation formula is:

$$
\Delta t = \gamma \Delta \tau
$$

where

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
$$

## Step 1: Find the Lorentz Factor

Substitute the given values:

$$
2 = \gamma(1)
$$

Therefore:

$$
\gamma = 2
$$

## Step 2: Solve for Velocity

Using the Lorentz factor equation:

$$
2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
$$

Take the reciprocal of both sides:

$$
\sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}} = \frac{1}{2}
$$

Square both sides:

$$
1-\frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{1}{4}
$$

Rearrange:

$$
\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - \frac{1}{4}
$$

$$
\frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{3}{4}
$$

Take the square root:

$$
\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{\frac{3}{4}}
$$

$$
\frac{v}{c} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
v \approx 0.866c
$$
