# ⚖️ Linear Momentum Comparison Analysis

This study provides a detailed mathematical comparison of the linear momentum between two objects with significantly different masses and velocities: a **housefly** and a **tennis ball**.

## 1. Physical Principles
Linear momentum ($\vec{p}$) is a vector quantity defined as the product of an object's mass ($m$) and its velocity ($\vec{v}$). 

The fundamental formula is:
$$\vec{p} = m \cdot \vec{v}$$

### Key Constraints:
* **Mass ($m$):** Must be expressed in Kilograms ($kg$) for SI consistency.
* **Velocity ($v$):** Must be expressed in Meters per second ($m/s$).
* **Unit of Momentum:** Newton-seconds ($N \cdot s$) or $kg \cdot m/s$.

---

## 2. Detailed Mathematical Calculations

### Case A: The Fly
Given:
* $m_{fly} = 2 \text{ g}$
* $v_{fly} = 10 \text{ m/s}$

**Step 1: Unit Conversion**
To convert grams to kilograms, we use the factor $10^{-3}$:
$$m_1 = 2 \text{ g} \times \frac{1 \text{ kg}}{1000 \text{ g}} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$$

**Step 2: Momentum Calculation**
$$p_1 = m_1 \cdot v_1$$
$$p_1 = 0.002 \text{ kg} \cdot 10 \text{ m/s}$$
$$p_1 = \mathbf{0.02 \text{ kg} \cdot m/s}$$



---

### Case B: The Tennis Ball
Given:
* $m_{ball} = 60 \text{ g}$
* $v_{ball} = 1 \text{ m/s}$

**Step 1: Unit Conversion**
$$m_2 = 60 \text{ g} \times \frac{1 \text{ kg}}{1000 \text{ g}} = 0.060 \text{ kg}$$

**Step 2: Momentum Calculation**
$$p_2 = m_2 \cdot v_2$$
$$p_2 = 0.060 \text{ kg} \cdot 1 \text{ m/s}$$
$$p_2 = \mathbf{0.06 \text{ kg} \cdot m/s}$$

---

## 3. Comparative Analysis

To determine the magnitude of difference, we calculate the ratio ($R$):
$$R = \frac{p_{ball}}{p_{fly}} = \frac{0.06}{0.02} = 3$$

### Observation:
Even though the fly is moving **10 times faster** than the tennis ball, the tennis ball's mass is **30 times greater** than the fly's mass. Because momentum is linearly proportional to both variables, the mass difference dominates the result.

**Result:** The **Tennis Ball** has **3 times** greater momentum than the fly.
